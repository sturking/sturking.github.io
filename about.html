About Me:

Hi, I'm Sophia, the creator of this website. I'm majoring in cybersecurity at Kennesaw State University, and I'm on track to graduate in Summer 2026. I've held a 3.98 GPA and made the President's List every semester, and I became a member of Upsilon Pi Epsilon, the international honor society for computing. I'm currently enrolled in IT 3203: Introduction to Web Development, Section W01, Summer semester 2026.

My experience mostly revolves around spotting problems and defending against them. I've managed a digital forensics case entirely in Autopsy, handling evidence acquisition, timeline reconstruction, and final documentation. I led a team through an incident-response tabletop exercise as the Incident Response Manager. My work has included weighing SIEM platforms, rebuilding firewall, VPN, and DNS architecture for a case organization, and configuring and hardening both Linux and Windows servers by hand from the command line. These projects have helped me become familiar with industry-standard tools such as Wireshark, Nmap, Autopsy, and Sysinternals, as well as with frameworks such as NIST CSF 2.0, NIST SP 800-61, and MITRE ATT&CK.

For the past few years, I've also been working as a Client Care Specialist at a veterinary hospital alongside my coursework. My role involves managing and maintaining more than 10,000 client records and serving as the first point of contact for questions or concerns. That kind of work has sharpened my eye for detail and taught me how to trace a problem back to its real source. I carry that curiosity about technology and that commitment to getting the details right into every project, including the research you'll find here on how web browsers and the web have evolved.

This is a class project for IT 3203.
